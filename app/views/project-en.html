1. Project objectives: Why such work has been undertaken?

The first objective aims at identifying the research centers of the discipline. In a segmented institutional landscape - between the Ministry of National Education of Higher Education and Research (MENESR) and the National Center for Scientific Research (CNRS) or between Universities and Institutes of Political Studies (IEP) - there was no common tool for identifying research centers "belonging" to political science. Establishing such a list may encounter difficulties and we will go on to detail in the methodological part the continuous work of adjustment that has been necessary and is continuing.

Beyond the centers, it is also a question of identifying the persons (directors of laboratories, directors of Doctoral Schools, resource persons in documentation) to facilitate the flow of information and networking.

Identifying the research themes is a central element of this work that can benefit the greatest number of users interested in political science. It is also a strategic element from a library point of view. These qualitative data collected are intended to help develop an acquisitions policy, that is in line with the current lines of research, particularly in terms of digital resources. Some acquisitions can already take into account emerging research themes. Once completed and regularly updated, they can help monitor subscriptions to digital resources.

This mapping includes an important library component aimed at identifying the resources and library practices within the research centers. This will help to better identify library needs and deploy appropriate services to serve the identified research community. This library dimension is explained by the context that gave birth to this project.

2. Context of the project

The project is part of the CollEx ("Collections of Excellence") infrastructure, a national library cooperation system, born from the need to renovate the Centers for the Acquisition and Dissemination of Scientific and Technical Information (CADIST) which structured the library network of Higher Education and Research (ESR) for 30 years.

The SciencesPo library was CADIST in political science since 1982. As such, its mission was to acquire, preserve and disseminate resources in this discipline: research-level documents, French and foreign, in all media.

CollEx-Persée took the form of a Group of Scientific Interest (GIS) for a period of 6 years (2017-2022). It aims to contribute to the performance of French research by developing library services closer to the needs of researchers.

By coordinating, enriching the content offer and developing services, CollEx-Persée intends to meet a double challenge: easy and widely accessible access to library resources; expert management of data and documents related to research programs.

It brings together, alongside Persée, the main actors of IST in France:

  • operators supporting national IST policies (ABES, CTLES)
  • national structures with large heritage collections (BnF, INIST)
  • CollEx Delegate Libraries
  • CollEx Associated Libraries

At the end of the call for expressions of interest, the Sciences Po library was one of the 9 institutions selected in 2017 and is CollEx's "delegatee library" for political science.

Thus, the basic allocation usually received for library acquisitions was partially replaced by a project grant. Since these projects must be part of a research service approach, the Sciences Po library therefore proposed the development of this mapping as an initial project. Capturing the "community" of researchers in the discipline is a prerequisite for projects in order to improve  the tools and services of a CollEx library intended for the latter.

3. Project realization: methodology

Elaboration of the list of centers

The plural nature of political science materializes even in spelling. For Sciences Po and the Ecole Libre des Sciences Politiques, the plural is used. On the other hand, when we talk about the academic subject, the singular is privileged. It is also the result of an intellectual and institutional construction that has distinguished itself from other disciplines (history, law, and even more recently philosophy) accompanied by more and more important connexions with  sociology and humanities and social sciences in general. The major role of political sociology within the discipline bears witness to this (you will find at the end of this page a bibliography on the history of political science, its institutionalization and its current landscape).

The subject of the discipline is not obvious and this is reflected both in the research themes but also in the difficulties to delimit the boundaries, including institutional, of the discipline.

Taken at the same time in a multidisciplinary logic and in the development of sub-disciplines, it is not surprising that it is difficult to establish the outlines of the discipline.

When we set up the project of mapping research centers in political science, we had the greatest difficulty in defining which centers belonged to the discipline despite the "objective" criteria that we sought to use.

To establish the list of centers to be treated, we cross-checked :

  • The list of Research Units, having political science as "main discipline" in the Annuaire des formations doctorales et des unités de recherche of the Ministry of National Education, Higher Education and Research associated with the Répertoire National des Structures de Recherche (RNSR)
  • The research centers of section 40 "Politics, power, organization" of the CNRS (National Center for Scientific Research) Annuaire des laboratoires et des personnels
  • The list extracted from the Annuaire des laboratoires et des personnels of the CNRS for research centers that have a thematic indexing that contains: "SC.HS - SCIENCES POLITIQUES"
  • L’annuaire des laboratoires en science politique from the ANCMSP

Centers not selected

  • Centers present abroad have not been selected. Only the centers present in France have been selected.
  • Centers that did not wish to appear or were not considered relevant in political science were not included (no research topics and no political science researchers).
  • For the “Maison des Sciences de l'Homme sur l'environnement”, we have retained only their research center in political science: Centre de recherches juridiques de l'université de Franche-Comté (CRJFC).

It is also possible that we come to integrate or withdraw centers that request it. It is now possible to make these requests for additions, withdrawals or updates directly from the site. We are thinking of setting up a scientific committee representing the different institutions and spectrum of political science which would be responsible in particular for defining the perimeter of the centers treated in the cartography.

Collection of data

Data collection was conducted successively by three PhDs or doctoral students in political science following the same method.

From the pre-established list, two complementary techniques made it possible to collect more or less complete and detailed information: first the collection by web sites dedicated to political science  and then the verification of the data collected by phone interviews (for units outside the Ile-de-France) or physical (in Ile-de-France), when this was possible. Indeed, it is regrettable that our approach (and particularly the solicitation of interviews) has not always received a favorable reception.

In order to establish the detailed sheet of each center on the five axes (Description: Doctoral Schools, Thematics, Publications, Documentation), the collection started with the systematic analysis of the websites of the research centers. These collected data were then supplemented, when possible, by a set of references and elements available either on the virtual reference space of the Research Unit on the MENESR directory (doctoral schools, discipline main / secondary discipline, keywords, etc.), or on the virtual reference area of the CNRS directory (personnel data, membership sections of researchers, etc.).

It is only at the end of this first part of the process, essential to the structuring of an informed discourse on the research unit, that an interview with one of the resource persons of the research center was solicited - usually but not exclusively its director. The verification of the collected data and the collection of the missing data were the objectives of this second moment of our approach.

Any request for interview was made through e-mail (once or several times in some cases) around three arguments: first the presentation of the project; then the subject of the interview; and finally the relevance of such an approach. Sometimes we have been redirected to other resource persons than those originally contacted. Generally, the motivations of this reorientation are summarized around the qualification or the legitimacy to express oneself. To cover all the themes of the data to be collected, it has often been necessary to interview two or three different people. Thus, the information relative to the Doctoral Schools was obtained from specific contacts. The same for data concerning library resources: when the center had a documentation center, its manager was systematically contacted on these questions.

On the opposite, we were silenced by many people contacted: either by a lack of response, or by the expression of a desire not to participate in the survey. In the interest of transparency, when a data is missing, we opted for an identification of the deliberate silence of the interlocutors in our collection by the mention "NC" (Not Communicated), this in order to distinguish it from the total absence information, symbolized by X.

4. Description of the data: What questions does this website answer?

The fact sheets of the research centers are based on 5 axes: Description; Doctoral schools; Themes; Publications; Documentation.

Description

It provides information on institutional information: on management, location, contacts, and related institutions. There is also a link to the research center's website.

For staff, three figures are given: total staff, permanent staff and non-permanent staff. We have chosen not to take into account the different statuses (lecturers, professors, research officers, research directors, FNSP or FMSH statutes, etc.) and to keep permanent / non-permanent as the only variable. Finally, to know in detail the composition of the workforce, we have indicated the links to the lists of researchers on the website of the center and the CNRS site.

All the official information that we have mentioned so far has been collected from the CNRS and MENESR directories and verified during the interviews.

In addition to the year of creation, we also put a text presentation of the history coming mostly from the center's website. Concerning the year of creation: in the case of a merger, we have chosen to keep the creation date of the new entity, and not the date of creation of the oldest center (example: CESSP. 2010). However, when it was the same entity only changing its name, we kept the oldest creation date.

Doctoral schools

This part indicates which doctoral school(s) the center is attached to. The name and code of these Schools, their directorate and their contacts are mentioned. We also find data on the number of PhD students, the number of PhD students in Political Science specifically (when it was possible to have the information) and the number of theses supported the previous year. All this information was collected on the one hand on the directory of the MENESR and on the other hand with interviews with the Doctoral Schools

Research themes

This part is obviously central in our approach and we tried to detail as much as possible.

Regarding the institutional classifications, we retained the CNRS sections and keywords in the MENESR directory.

On the lines of research, we have recovered those established by the centers. We also collected the research contracts that come mostly from arrow financings. This mode of operation being more and more frequent, it also provides information on the themes of these researches. Concerning the institutional classifications, we retained the sections of the CNRS and the keywords of the directory of the MENESR.

We also indicated all the titles of the research seminars that we were able to collect. These seminars are a reflection of the research being done and the themes developed and taught by the researchers of these laboratories.

Finally, we asked if there were networks and collaborations around research projects.

All this information (with the exception of institutional data) was collected either on the websites of the research centers or directly during the interviews.

Publications

What is in question here is not to provide or simply to indicate the publications but to highlight the modes and places of dissemination of research publications.

To do this, we indicate whether the center has book collections from publishers, whether it has its own journals or working papers series (including those published only online.

In addition it is indicated if the publications are deposit or not in HAL repository (the multidisciplinary open archive of the CNRS) and / or in an institutional repository. This is accompanied by a qualitative description of the recommendations and practices of the laboratory in terms of filing open-access publications.

We also collected information about research data and its archiving. It is clear that few centers have indicated that they have any action in this area. This confirms that this practice is still in its infancy in France.

Finally, in the last section on the valorisation of publications by the laboratory, we indicated if there was a space dedicated to this on the website of the laboratory, RSS feeds, newsletters, twitter accounts, working paper publications etc.

For this whole part, all the information gathered was almost entirely from the interviews. Which makes it an original contribution. It should be noted that there are publications and research data in this area that are highly disparate between laboratories in terms of funding, resources and practices.

Documentation

This last part also comes almost exclusively from interviews within the centers and with librarians (when the centers have them). As part of a project carried by a library within the framework of CollEx, information on library resources is obviously crucial.

We therefore collected information on the libraries used (only directly with the centers, without interrogating the researchers themselves). We must make it clear at the outset that we have not detailed the nature of the libraries used. This aspect could be developed in a future evolution of this mapping work (see below).

Always in the same mode, we also asked what are - broadly - digital resources available to researchers. This point could also be refined in future versions.

When there is a resource person from the library we have indicated the  name and the  contact.

The following data relate to the libraries of the research centers. When they have them, we have collected information about the name of the library and its website (or web page), the nature of its collection, its library policy, and the library training offers available to researchers.

Finally, we have also mentioned the library collaborations that may exist (even on small ecosystems).

It should be noted that, in addition to the large disparities in terms of size and resources between the different research centers, since data on documentary resources is based almost exclusively on interviews, there is a strong imbalance in the information collected. It was easier when we had librarians for the interviews. It is clear that often we either lacked interlocutors, or those we had in research centers were sometimes not very sensitive or very familiar with library issues

First observations

Although we have not yet made any real analyses at this stage, some observations emerge from this survey.

First what is obvious in observing the map are geographical disparities. And especially the predominant place of Paris. This situation can be explained historically, in particular by the place occupied in the institutionalization of political science in France by the FNSP and Paris 1. And even if the AFSP (Association Française de science Politique) surveys show a downward trend in the weight of research centers and Parisian researchers in the discipline, we observe that the center of gravity of the discipline remains strongly marked.

From the institutional point of view, there is a great variety in the size of the research centers (especially in terms of staff). But also great differences between centers turned almost exclusively towards political science (even in small numbers) and other centers more important in terms of staff, often multidisciplinary, but where political science occupies only a marginal place.

On staff, it can be noted that the total number of staff includes only a minority share of permanent staff. Even if the data do not allow us to grasp diachronic evolutions, there is every reason to believe that the development of research contracts and project research increases the proportion of non-permanent staff (in particular doctoral or post-doctoral staff) in the overall size of the research centers.

With regard to the research themes (see word cloud), we observe that, as the discipline in general, the themes of political philosophy and constitutional law are less and less present contrary to themes in political sociology or international relations.

We also note the development of themes related to the analysis of public policies (on health, urban policies, on discrimination). There is also a trend towards other multidisciplinary themes: gender or environmental studies.

It can be hypothesised that the development of these "new" themes is based on the increasing share of project-based contracts in the funding of research.

This is not without consequences on the themes that must be privileged in library policies concerning acquisitions. There is a need here to adapt the library collections to the developments in political science.

With regard to the archiving of research data, it is obvious that although the collection of information may have been incomplete, the limited data collected on this matter reveals how much this field is still more than stammering in France. Moreover, this practice is of little institutional significance. It could be interesting that support for research through the development of CollEx services also addresses this issue.

On open archive publications, it is interesting to note that the practice of filing in HAL is still far from the rule. Difference between the recommendations of laboratories and the practices of researchers. Some laboratories complain that researchers can more easily put their work online on social networks of research (Academia, Research Gate) rather than depositing them in open-access warehouses.

Our survey also shows that there is still a large share of research libraries within the research centers. Seeing them listed in this tool makes it possible to better understand their funds and activities. Some "library niches" could thus be detected on thematic funds.

To be able to support these first observations and to analyze them thoroughly, it would be necessary to follow a scientific method: to question, to quantify, to argue, to check.

It is also for this reason that we have wished for the data of this cartography to be shared with the greatest number, in the hope notably that researchers are appropriating it in the framework of researches on political science as a discipline

5. Dissemination and updates

This website, which provides a visualization tool for the data collected, was developed in partnership between the Sciences Po library and the medialab. This map is also available on the AFSP website and will be available on the CollEx website.

All data presented are freely downloadable. Provision of complete data (or selections from your research) in the form of a download link data in CSV format. The data is then reusable freely.

The question of updating the data is strategic for this type of tool. If the map is not updated at least once a year, it will quickly become irrelevant. To facilitate this update we have developed a Back Office based on a perennial database that can be supplemented by a form.

In this way, research centers can autonomously update their data. In addition, we want to join a collaborative approach to improve this tool. Everyone can submit corrections or updates. These requests must be validated by one of the administrators to be published on the site.

The “Association Française de Science Politique”(AFSP) has agreed to be a stakeholder in this updating endeavour and has a status of administrator.

In addition, we also carry out a data backup each year in order to possibly allow diachronic analyzes. Thus it will be possible to show the evolution over time of such and such data. These backups have not yet been published on the site but can be provided upon request.

6. Tracks of developments

It seems important to us to set up a scientific committee. It will be able to guide choices in terms of research centers to be included in the cartography (the boundaries of the discipline being, as we have seen, very subjective). But also to determine the relevant evolutions, highlight the types of data to harvest that can be added, provide research leads, adjust the methodology, etc.

Collaboration and sharing of certain data could also be developed with the ScanR tool "research and innovation engine" across all disciplines in France, put online by the MENESR in July 2016 Their (more quantitative) approach and ours can certainly find points of synergy to improve both tools.

Another development could also be of great help. It seems important to create a repository of controlled keywords for research topics. Beyond the provision of structured information facilitating navigation on the map, this would allow to identify recurrences and make more refined analyzes.

Finally, even if this cartography was first conceived from the research centers, it could be intended to integrate the network of libraries that have a collection in political science (University Libraries, IEP libraries, BNF). For the moment, only the libraries used are mentioned, and only the collections and activities of the documentation centers in the laboratories have been collected.
